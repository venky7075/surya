Bee Sting First Aid

1. Remove the Stinger

  • Scrape it out: Use a fingernail or the blunt edge of a credit card.
  • Avoid squeezing: Do not use tweezers, as this may release more venom.

2. Clean the Area

  • Wash with soap and water to prevent infection.

3. Apply a Cold Compress

  • Apply an ice pack wrapped in a cloth for 10-20 minutes to reduce swelling.

4. Other Helpful Measures

  • Elevate the affected limb to reduce swelling.
  • Take over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen.
  • Use antihistamines for itching.
  • Apply calamine lotion or hydrocortisone cream for relief.

5. Precautions After a Bee Sting

  • Avoid scratching the area to prevent infection.
  • Drink plenty of water to help flush out toxins.
  • Stay indoors for a while to avoid getting stung again.
  • If you have a known bee allergy, carry an epinephrine injector (EpiPen).
